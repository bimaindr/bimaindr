### 3. Bike Sharing Analysisi
Urban mobility and bike-sharing systems have grown significantly, making efficient fleet management critical. The primary challenge is predicting and understanding demand fluctuations. Without data-driven insights, bike-sharing companies struggle with uneven bike distribution (e.g., shortages during rush hours or oversupply during bad weather). 

This project aims to solve that problem by answering core business questions:
1. **Time-Based Demand:** How do seasonal, monthly, and hourly patterns affect the total volume of bike rentals?
2. **User Segmentation:** What are the key operational differences between casual riders and registered members in terms of peak usage times?
3. **Environmental Impact:** How drastically do weather variations (temperature, wind speed, and humidity) impact user rental behavior?

## ⚙️ Tech Stack
* **Language:** Python
* **Data Libraries:** Pandas, NumPy
* **Visualization:** Matplotlib, Seaborn
* **Dashboard & Deployment:** Streamlit, Streamlit Cloud

## 💻 What I Did (Data Analytics Workflow)
As the Data Analyst for this project, I executed the full end-to-end data pipeline:

* **Data Wrangling:** * **Gathering:** Loaded and inspected the daily (`day.csv`) and hourly (`hour.csv`) bike-sharing datasets.
  * **Assessing:** Identified missing values, duplicates, and mismatched data types (such as converting date columns to the correct `datetime` format).
  * **Cleaning:** Handled inconsistencies and mapped categorical integers (seasons, weather conditions, holidays) into descriptive string labels for cleaner visualizations.

* **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):**
  * Uncovered critical insights showing that **Registered Users** heavily drive peak demand at 8:00 AM and 5:00 PM (commuting patterns), while **Casual Users** ride consistently throughout afternoon hours on weekends (leisure patterns).
  * Analyzed seasonality trends, proving that bike rentals peak significantly during the Summer/Fall seasons and hit their lowest point during Winter.

* **Data Visualization & Dashboard Engineering:**
  * Developed clear, interactive time-series plots and bar charts to display monthly and environmental metrics.
  * Engineered an interactive web application utilizing **Streamlit**, enabling users to dynamically filter data by date range and view real-time KPIs (Total Users, Registered vs Casual breakdown, and environmental correlations).
